# Standards

## CISA De-escalation Guidelines

Based on the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency De-escalation Action Guide (2024).

## Core Principles

### 1. Prioritize Safety
- Safety of self, subject, and others is the first priority
- Position yourself with an exit available
- Maintain comfortable distance (arm's length minimum)
- Don't block the person's exit

### 2. Stay Calm
- Your calm can regulate their emotional state
- Speak slowly, quietly, deliberately
- Lower the pitch of your voice
- Avoid sudden movements
- Keep body language open and non-threatening

### 3. Active Listening
- Let them talk without interruption
- Don't argue or debate
- Reflect back what you hear
- Ask clarifying questions
- Show genuine interest in their concerns

### 4. Acknowledge
- Validate their feelings and concerns
- Show empathy
- Don't dismiss or minimize
- "I understand this is frustrating"

### 5. Problem-Solving Focus
- Shift from blame to solutions
- What can be done now?
- Offer limited choices
- Break problems into manageable parts

## The VERB Model

### Validate
Show that you understand and accept their feelings:
- "I understand this is frustrating"
- "I can see why you'd be upset"
- "Your concerns are valid"

### Empathize
Demonstrate understanding of their perspective:
- "If I were in your situation..."
- "I can imagine how frustrating this must be"
- "That sounds really difficult"

### Reframe
Shift focus from blame to solutions:
- "Let's figure out how we can move forward"
- "What would help resolve this?"
- "Here's what I can do"

### Border Setting
Establish clear, reasonable limits:
- "I can help with X, but Y isn't possible"
- "Here's what I can offer"
- "We need to keep this respectful"

---

## The GAIN Method

### Gather
Collect information:
- What's happening?
- What's upset them?
- What do they want?

### Acknowledge
Validate feelings:
- Show you understand
- Don't dismiss or minimize

### Identify
Find the core issue:
- What's the real problem?
- What's driving their frustration?

### Negotiate
Work toward solutions:
- What can be done?
- What's realistic?

---

## When to Escalate

Call authorities or security when:
- Physical threats to self or others
- Weapon displayed or mentioned
- Person becomes physically aggressive
- Situation exceeds your ability to manage
- You feel unsafe
