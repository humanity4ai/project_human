# Handoff

## When to Involve Others

### Immediate Escalation (Call 911/Security)
- Physical threats to self or others
- Weapon displayed or mentioned
- Physical aggression
- Person is not responsive to de-escalation
- You feel unsafe
- Person is intoxicated/impaired

### Supervisor Escalation
- You've tried de-escalation without success
- Customer specifically requests supervisor
- Policy exception needed
- Situation requires authority
- Legal/compliance issues

### Peer Support
- Need a second voice
- Taking turns reduces fatigue
- Fresh perspective helpful

---

## How to Hand Off

### To Supervisor
1. Brief them first: "Situation is X, we've tried Y"
2. Give them context
3. Stay available as backup
4. Don't abandon the customer

### To Security
1. Alert them quietly
2. Don't draw attention
3. Have them nearby but not intrusive
4. Stay calm yourself

### To 911
1. Speak clearly
2. Give location
3. Describe situation
4. Follow instructions

---

## Handoff Language

### To Customer
- "I'm going to get my supervisor to help"
- "I'm bringing in someone who can help more"
- "Let me connect you with someone who has more authority"

### From Supervisor
- "Hi, I'm [name], I'll be helping you now"
- "I've been briefed on the situation"
- "Here's what I can do..."

---

## After Handoff

- Document what happened
- Note what worked/didn't
- Debrief if possible
- Take care of yourself
- Report if needed
