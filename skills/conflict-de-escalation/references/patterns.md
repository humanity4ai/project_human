# Patterns

## Common Escalation Triggers

### Verbal Triggers
- Dismissive language ("calm down")
- Threatening consequences
- Sarcasm or mockery
- Interrupting
- Talking over them
- "You" statements that blame
- Absolute statements ("you always," "you never")
- Dismissing their concern

### Non-Verbal Triggers (if visible)
- Crossing arms
- Leaning in too close
- Making sudden movements
- Rolling eyes
- Looking away dismissively
- Tense body posture
- Pointing fingers

### Situational Triggers
- Long wait times
- Feeling ignored or dismissed
- Being transferred repeatedly
- Perceived unfair treatment
- Lack of control/choices

---

## De-escalation Patterns That Work

### Validation Phrases
- "I understand this is frustrating"
- "I can see why you'd be upset"
- "Your concerns are valid"
- "That sounds really difficult"
- "I would feel the same way"

### Empathy Statements
- "I'm sorry you've been through this"
- "I can imagine how frustrating this is"
- "This has been a hard experience for you"
- "I hear you"

### Solution-Focused Language
- "Here's what I can do..."
- "Let's figure this out together"
- "What would help most right now?"
- "I can help you with..."

### Boundary Setting
- "I can help with X, but Y isn't possible"
- "I need you to speak respectfully"
- "Here's what I can offer"
- "We need to work together on this"

## Patterns That Escalate

### Dismissive
- "Calm down"
- "You're overreacting"
- "That's not a big deal"
- "Everyone has to wait"

### Blaming
- "You should have..."
- "You never..."
- "This is your fault"
- "If you hadn't..."

### Threatening
- "If you don't..."
- "You're going to..."
- "I'll have to call..."
- "There will be consequences"

### Dismissive Authority
- "That's not my policy"
- "I can't make exceptions"
- "There's nothing I can do"
- "That's not my department"

## TheEAR model

### E - Engage
Make contact respectfully:
- Use their name if known
- Open body language
- Gentle tone

### A - Ask
Gather information:
- Open-ended questions
- Listen actively
- Clarify concerns

### R - Reflect
Show understanding:
- Paraphrase back
- Validate feelings
- Show empathy
