# Techniques

## Verbal De-escalation Techniques

### 1. Lower Your Voice
- Speak slower than normal
- Use quiet volume
- Lower pitch of voice
- Calm voice helps regulate their emotion

### 2. Use Their Name
- Personalizes interaction
- Shows respect
- Creates connection
- "Mr. Smith, I want to help you"

### 3. Minimal Encouragers
- "I see," "Go on," "I understand"
- Shows you're listening
- Encourages venting
- Doesn't add content

### 4. Reflection
- Paraphrase what they said
- "So what I'm hearing is..."
- Shows understanding
- Can clarify misunderstandings

### 5. Strategic Silence
- Don't fill every pause
- Let them fill gaps
- Shows you're thinking
- Creates space for reflection

### 6. "I" Statements
- "I can help you with..."
- "I understand..."
- "I want to find a solution"
- Avoids blame

### 7. Limited Choices
- "Would you prefer A or B?"
- Gives sense of control
- Reduces helplessness
- Still within your bounds

### 8. Acknowledgment Before Response
- Always acknowledge first
- Then address content
- "I hear that, and here's what I can do..."

## Physical Techniques (if visible)

### Body Positioning
- Stay at eye level
- Don't tower over
- Keep distance (arm's length)
- Keep hands visible
- Open body language

### Movement
- Move slowly
- Avoid sudden movements
- Don't corner them
- Keep exit path clear

## Crisis De-escalation

### For High Emotion
1. Stay calm (your regulation helps them)
2. Don't take it personally
3. Acknowledge first
4. Set clear limits
5. Offer choices

### For Threats
1. Don't argue or debate
2. Set clear boundary: "I need you to speak respectfully"
3. Offer one path forward
4. Know when to exit
5. Call for help

### For Weapon
1. Do not attempt to disarm
2. Stay calm
3. Comply with demands if safe
4. Create distance
5. Call 911 immediately

## Key Phrases

### To Validate
- "I understand this is frustrating"
- "I can see why you'd be upset"
- "Your concerns are important to me"

### To Empathize
- "I'm sorry this happened to you"
- "This must be really difficult"
- "I would feel frustrated too"

### To Problem-Solve
- "Here's what I can do..."
- "Let's work together on this"
- "What's the most important thing we can fix now?"

### To Set Boundaries
- "I need you to speak respectfully"
- "I can help with X but not Y"
- "Here's what I can offer"
