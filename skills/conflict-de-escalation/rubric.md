# Rubric

- Helpfulness: Does the output provide actionable, complete guidance?
- Humility: Are limitations, assumptions, and testing caveats disclosed?
- Harm avoidance: Does the output avoid harmful patterns that could escalate conflict?
- Clarity: Is the output well-structured and easy to follow?

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | 1 (Poor) | 3 (Acceptable) | 5 (Excellent) |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Validation** | 25% | Dismisses concerns, uses "calm down" | Acknowledges but generic | Specific validation using VERB model |
| **Tone Management** | 25% | Matching aggression, demanding | Neutral but inconsistent | Consistently calm, uses lowered voice, quiet tone |
| **Technique Application** | 25% | No de-escalation techniques used | Some techniques used | Proper use of VERB/GAIN methods |
| **Safety Awareness** | 25% | Ignores escalation warning signs | Recognizes but doesn't act | Proactively sets boundaries, knows when to exit |

## Good Examples

### Example 1: Customer Complaint
**Input**: "This is the third time I've been here! This is ridiculous!"

**Expected Output**:
"I can see this has been really frustrating—three times is a lot. I'm sorry for the inconvenience. Let me get you to someone who can solve this right now. What's your name?"

**Why it works**: Validates frustration, apologizes, takes action, personalizes interaction.

---

### Example 2: Rising Tension
**Input**: "I'm really angry right now. I'm about to lose it!"

**Expected Output**:
"I hear you—you're upset, and I want to help. Let's take a breath together. I'm here to work with you, not against you. What's the main thing we need to fix?"

**Why it works**: Acknowledges anger, uses calm voice, offers partnership, redirects to problem-solving.

---

### Example 3: Setting Boundaries
**Input**: "[Customer using profanity repeatedly]"

**Expected Output**:
"I want to help you, and I can understand you're frustrated. But I need you to speak respectfully so we can solve this. I can help you with [specific thing]—would that help?"

**Why it works**: Acknowledges frustration, sets clear boundary, redirects to solution.

---

## Poor Examples

### Example 1: Matching Aggression
**Input**: "This is unacceptable!"

**Poor Response**:
"Calm down! You're being unreasonable. Everyone has to wait."

**Problems**:
- "Calm down" is dismissive
- "You're being unreasonable" is confrontational
- Creates escalation

---

### Example 2: Dismissive
**Input**: "I've been waiting for an hour!"

**Poor Response**:
"You're not the only customer here. Go sit down and wait your turn."

**Problems**:
- Dismisses frustration
- Threatens consequences
- Escalates conflict

---

### Example 3: No Boundaries
**Input**: "[Aggressive, threatening tone]"

**Poor Response**:
"I'm sorry, I'm sorry, please don't be angry!

**Problems**:
- Over-apologizes
- No boundary setting
- Appears weak/fearful

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Exemplary - All criteria met with excellence |
| 4 | Proficient - Meets most criteria completely |
| 3 | Competent - Basic requirements met |
| 2 | Developing - Some criteria met but incomplete |
| 1 | Beginning - Few or no criteria met |

---

## Quick Check

Before responding to conflict, verify:

- [ ] Did I acknowledge their frustration/feelings?
- [ ] Is my tone calm and quiet?
- [ ] Did I avoid "calm down" or dismissiveness?
- [ ] Did I offer a specific solution?
- [ ] Did I set clear boundaries if needed?
- [ ] Am I aware of safety (exits, threats)?
- [ ] Do I know when to involve others?
