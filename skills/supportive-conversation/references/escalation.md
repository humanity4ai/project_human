# Escalation Protocols

## When to Escalate

Escalation means connecting the person with professional help. Always escalate when:

### Immediate Escalation (Emergency Services)
- Clear suicide plan with intent
- Access to means (pills, weapons, etc.)
- Time frame set (today, tonight)
- Final goodbyes or giving away possessions
- Acting dangerously

**Action**: Call emergency services (911, 999) or encourage them to

### High Priority Escalation
- Suicidal thoughts without specific plan
- Self-harm mentions
- Severe depression/anxiety impacting daily life
- Expressions of hopelessness
- Feeling like a burden

**Action**: Provide crisis resources (988, Crisis Text Line), encourage immediate contact

### Suggest Professional Help (Ask First)
- Ongoing struggles (more than 2 weeks)
- Difficulty functioning (work, relationships, self-care)
- Person asks for "real help"
- Situations beyond your scope
- Recurring emotional patterns

**Action**: Suggest resources, ask if they want them

### Standard Support (No Escalation Needed)
- Normal emotional responses
- Venting/validation needed
- Life transitions/stressors
- Looking for connection not solutions

**Action**: Standard supportive response

## How to Escalate

### 1. Normalize the Conversation

Before introducing resources, normalize:
- "What you're going through is really hard"
- "It makes sense you're feeling this way"
- "Thank you for trusting me with this"

### 2. Introduce Resources Naturally

- "I want to make sure you have support"
- "There are people who are really good at helping with this"
- "Would you be willing to talk to someone?"

### 3. Provide Specific Resources

Don't just say "get help." Provide:
- Specific phone numbers (988)
- Specific text lines (HOME to 741741)
- Websites they can visit

### 4. Make It Easy

- Offer to stay on the phone while they call
- Offer to help them find local resources
- Give them the number even if they decline right now

### 5. Respect Autonomy

- Don't force: "Will you call?" not "You must call"
- Provide resources even if they decline
- "Here's the number in case you need it later"

## Escalation Language

### Introducing Resources
```
"I want to make sure you have support. There's this number 988—"

"I've found that talking to someone really helps. Would you be willing to—"

"There's a text line where you can talk to someone. It's 741741, text HOME—"

"I care about you, and I want to make sure you're safe."
```

### If They Decline
```
"I understand. Would you be willing to keep this number somewhere, in case you need it later?"

"I respect that. Can we keep talking? I'm here."

"Just know that 988 is available anytime, even if you don't want to call right now."

"Would it help to have someone to talk to right now besides me?"
```

### If They're in Immediate Danger
```
"I'm really concerned about you right now. Can we get you some help?"

"I think we should call someone who can help keep you safe. Can we call 911 together?"

"I'm going to stay with you. Let's get you some help right now."
```

## Professional Resources to Suggest

### Crisis Lines
| Resource | Contact | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| 988 Lifeline | Call or text 988 | US, 24/7 |
| Crisis Text Line | Text HOME to 741741 | US, 24/7 |
| Samaritans | 116 123 | UK |
| Lifeline | 13 11 14 | Australia |
| Crisis Services Canada | 1-833-456-4566 | Canada |

### Professional Help
- **Therapists/Psychologists**: Psychology Today (psychologytoday.com)
- **Psychiatrists**: American Psychiatric Association (psychiatry.org)
- **Primary Care**: Can provide referrals
- **Employee Assistance Programs**: Check with employer
- **University Counseling**: If student
- **Community Mental Health**: Low-cost options

### Online Resources
- **7Cups**: https://www.7cups.com/
- **Talkspace**: https://www.talkspace.com/
- **BetterHelp**: https://www.bethelp.com/
- **National Institute of Mental Health**: https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/find-help

## Documentation

After escalation:
- Note what resources were provided
- Note person's response
- If emergency services were called, note that
- Follow up if appropriate
