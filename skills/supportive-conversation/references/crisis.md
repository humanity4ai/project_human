# Crisis Detection and Response

## Overview

Crisis response is the most critical part of this skill. When someone expresses suicidal ideation, self-harm thoughts, or severe emotional distress, your response can literally save their life. This guide covers detection, response, and resources.

---

## Risk Indicators

### Immediate Risk (Requires Emergency Response)
- Explicit suicide plans
- Access to means (pills, weapons, etc.)
- Time/place set
- Final goodbyes ("goodbye" messages, giving away possessions)
- Sudden calm after prolonged distress (may indicate decision made)

**Action**: Encourage immediate emergency services (911, 999, or local emergency number)

### High Risk (Requires Crisis Resources)
- Suicidal thoughts without specific plan
- Expressions of hopelessness
- "Everyone would be better off without me"
- "I don't want to be here anymore"
- Self-harm mentions
- Severe depression indicators

**Action**: Provide crisis resources, encourage immediate contact

### Moderate Risk (Requires Support + Resources)
- Ongoing emotional distress
- Difficulty functioning daily
- Isolating behavior
- Expressing worthlessness
- Significant life stressors

**Action**: Validate, provide resources, suggest professional help

### Low Risk (Standard Support)
- Normal emotional responses to difficult situations
- Looking for validation or venting
- Not expressing harm to self

**Action**: Standard supportive response

## Crisis Language Detection

### Direct Statements
```
"I want to kill myself"
"I'm going to end it"
"I want to die"
"I want the pain to stop"
```

### Indirect Statements
```
"I don't want to be here anymore"
"Everyone would be better off without me"
"I can't do this anymore"
"Nobody would miss me"
"It would be easier if I wasn't around"
"I feel like a burden"
"There's no point anymore"
"I just want the pain to stop"
```

### Behavioral Indicators (in conversation context)
- Sudden topic change to death/dying
- Questions about death methods
- Expressing being a "burden"
- Feeling like a "failure"
- Giving away possessions
- Saying goodbye unexpectedly

## Required Crisis Response

When ANY risk indicator is present, your response MUST include:

### 1. Validation
"I'm so glad you reached out to me. Thank you for trusting me with this."

### 2. Express Care
"I want you to know that you matter. Your life has value, even when it doesn't feel like it."

### 3. Provide Specific Resources

**United States**:
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**: Call or text **988**
- **Crisis Text Line**: Text **HOME** to **741741**
- **SAMHSA Helpline**: 1-800-662-4357

**International**:
- **IASP Crisis Centres**: https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/
- **Befrienders Worldwide**: https://www.befrienders.org/
- **UK Samaritans**: 116 123
- **Canada Crisis Lines**: https://www.crisisservicescanada.ca/
- **Australia Lifeline**: 13 11 14

### 4. Encourage Professional Help
"Would you be willing to talk to someone who can help right now?"

### 5. Stay With Them (If Appropriate)
"I'm going to stay here with you. Can you stay on the line while we get you some help?"

## Response Templates

### Template 1: Direct Suicidal Ideation
```
I'm so glad you reached out to me. Thank you for telling me this—I know it takes courage.

I want you to know that you matter, and I want to make sure you're safe right now.

Please reach out right now:
- Call or text 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
- Text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)

Would you be willing to call 988 or text HOME right now while we're talking? You don't have to face this alone.
```

### Template 2: Hopelessness
```
I'm hearing how much pain you're in, and I want you to know that I take this seriously. I'm so glad you reached out.

When things feel hopeless, it can be really hard to see a way forward. But I want you to know that this feeling can change, and there are people who can help.

Please consider:
- Calling 988 to talk to someone right now
- Texting HOME to 741741
- Going to your nearest emergency room

Would you be willing to reach out to one of these right now?
```

### Template 3: Self-Harm
```
Thank you for trusting me with this. I know it takes courage to talk about this.

I want to make sure you're safe. Have you hurt yourself, or do you have a plan to?

If you're in immediate danger, please call 911 or your local emergency number.

If you'd like to talk to someone right now:
- Call or text 988
- Text HOME to 741741

Can we get you some support right now?
```

```
        │
        ▼

- Not wanting to be here
        │
   ┌────┴────┐
   │ YES     │ NO
   ▼         ▼

```

## What NOT to Do

- **Never dismiss** the concern: "You're not really going to..."
- **Never argue** about the value of their life
- **Never lecture** about suicide being "wrong" or "sinful"
- **Never promise** to keep it secret (you may need to get help)
- **Never rush** to end the conversation
- **Never minimize**: "It can't be that bad"
- **Never play therapist** if you're not trained

---

## After Crisis Response

If the person engages with resources:
- Thank them for reaching out
- Offer to follow up
- Provide non-crisis resources for ongoing support

If the person declines help:
- Respect their autonomy
- Provide resources anyway ("Here's the number in case you need it later")
- Keep the conversation supportive
- Don't abandon them
