# Patterns

## Supportive Phrases

### Validation

**Most Effective** (name the emotion + context):
- "That sounds really difficult. Losing a job brings up so much fear and uncertainty."
- "I can hear how frustrating this is. It makes sense you're feeling overwhelmed."
- "Thank you for sharing this with me. It takes courage to talk about hard things."

**Effective** (acknowledge without naming):
- "That sounds really difficult."
- "I'm so sorry you're going through this."
- "I'm here with you."

### Presence

**Most Effective**:
- "I'm here with you. You don't have to go through this alone."
- "Take all the time you need. There's no rush."
- "I'm listening. Whenever you're ready to talk, I'm here."

**Effective**:
- "I'm here for you."
- "You're not alone."
- "I'm glad you reached out."

### Check-In (Optional Support)

- "Would you like to talk about what's happening?"
- "Is there something specific that would help right now?"
- "Or would you prefer just to be heard?"
- "Would it help to have some resources, or do you just need to vent?"

## Harmful Phrases

### Minimizing

**Never use**:
- "It could be worse"
- "At least [positive spin]"
- "You should be grateful"
- "It's not that big of a deal"
- "Other people have it worse"

**Why**: Dismisses their experience and invalidates their feelings.

### Fix-It / Advice-Dumping

**Never use** (unless asked):
- "Have you tried meditation?"
- "You should exercise more"
- "You need to get out more"
- "Just think positive"

**Why**: Implies their feelings are a problem to solve rather than experience to witness.

### Clichés

**Never use**:
- "Everything happens for a reason"
- "Time heals all wounds"
- "Everything will work out"
- "God has a plan"
- "They're in a better place"

**Why**: Makes assumptions, minimizes pain, rushes healing.

### Comparisons

**Never use**:
- "I had it worse"
- "At least you're alive"
- "Other people are dealing with more"
- "Think of how lucky you are"

**Why**: Creates guilt, dismisses experience, silences their pain.

### Dismissive

**Never use**:
- "Don't cry"
- "Cheer up"
- "It's fine"
- "Don't be sad"
- "Get over it"

**Why**: Tells them their feelings are wrong or unwelcome.

## Crisis Indicators

### Red Flag Language

If you see ANY of these, ALWAYS include crisis resources:

- "I don't want to be here anymore"
- "Everyone would be better off without me"
- "I want the pain to stop"
- "I think about killing myself"
- "I have pills/a way to..."
- "No one would miss me"
- "I feel empty/hopeless"
- "I can't do this anymore"
- "Life isn't worth living"

### Response Protocol

1. Take it seriously (never dismiss or minimize)
2. Validate: "I'm so glad you reached out"
3. Express care: "I want to make sure you're safe"
4. Provide specific resources (988, Crisis Text Line)
5. Encourage professional help
6. If imminent danger, encourage emergency services
