# Cultural Sensitivity — Standards and References

## Primary Standards

### APA Multicultural Guidelines (2017)
- Racial and Ethnocultural Identity: Guideline 1
- Intersectionality: Guideline 3
- Cultural Humility: Guideline 5
- URL: https://www.apa.org/about/policy/multicultural-guidelines

### UNESCO Universal Declaration on Cultural Diversity (2001)
- Article 1: Cultural diversity as common heritage of humanity
- Article 3: Cultural diversity as factor in development
- Article 5: Cultural rights as enabling environment for diversity
- URL: https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000127162

## Cultural Frameworks

### Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions (2001)
- Power Distance, Individualism-Collectivism, Masculinity-Femininity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-Term Orientation, Indulgence-Restraint
- Key critique: Ecological fallacy (national averages ≠ individual behavior) — McSweeney (2002), Human Relations, 55(1), 89-118
- Key critique: Western bias in dimension selection — Ailon (2008), Organization, 15(6), 885-902
- URL: https://geerthofstede.com/

### Schwartz Value Theory (2012)
- 10 basic personal values organized into 4 higher-order groups
- Empirically validated across 82 countries
- Schwartz, S.H. (2012). An overview of the Schwartz theory of basic values. Online Readings in Psychology and Culture, 2(1).

### World Values Survey
- Longitudinal study of cultural values across 120+ countries (1981-present)
- URL: https://www.worldvaluessurvey.org/

### GLOBE Study (2004)
- 9 cultural dimensions from 62 societies
- House, R.J. et al. (2004). Culture, Leadership, and Organizations: The GLOBE Study of 62 Societies. Sage.

## Color Semiotics (Multi-Regional)
- Western: White (purity), Black (mourning/formality), Red (danger/love)
- East Asian: White (mourning in China/Korea/Japan), Red (prosperity/luck)
- South Asian: Red (purity/marriage in India), White (mourning in Hindu traditions)
- African (diverse): Black (prosperity/maturity in some West African contexts), Red (mourning in South Africa)
- Middle Eastern: Green (Islam/sacred), Blue (protection)
- Indigenous Australian: Red, Yellow, Black (earth/land/sun in Aboriginal flag)

## Cultural Dimensions of UX

### Date and Time Formats
- ISO 8601: International date format standard (YYYY-MM-DD)
- Regional: DD/MM (UK, AU), MM/DD (US), YYYY年MM月DD日 (Japan, China)

### Name and Address Conventions
- Family name first: China, Japan, Korea, Hungary, Vietnam
- Given name first: Most Western countries
- Patronymic: Russia, Iceland, Arabic cultures
- No family name: Burma, Java, parts of South India

### Formality and Address
- Honorifics and titles: Pan/Pani (Polish), -san/-sama (Japanese), Usted (Spanish formal)
- Tu-vous distinction: French, Russian, Hindi, Tamil

## Indigenous and Decolonial Frameworks

### CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance
- Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics
- URL: https://www.gida-global.org/care

### Ubuntu Philosophy (Southern Africa)
- "I am because we are" — collective humanity, communal interdependence
- Inform UX choices for community-oriented digital experiences

## Limitations
- All cultural frameworks risk stereotyping when generalized to individuals
- Within-group variation often exceeds between-group variation
- Cultural norms evolve — frameworks should be re-validated periodically
