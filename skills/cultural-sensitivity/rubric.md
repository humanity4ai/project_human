# Rubric

- Helpfulness: Does the output provide actionable, complete guidance?
- Humility: Are limitations, assumptions, and testing caveats disclosed?
- Harm avoidance: Does the output avoid harmful patterns like cultural stereotypes?
- Clarity: Is the output well-structured and easy to follow?

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | 1 (Poor) | 3 (Acceptable) | 5 (Excellent) |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Language** | 25% | Uses idioms, slang | Clear language | Neutral, universally understandable |
| **Imagery** | 25% | Stereotypical | Some diversity | Fully inclusive representation |
| **Cultural Awareness** | 25% | Assumes universal norms | Some awareness | Considers cultural context |
| **Adaptability** | 25% | Single cultural view | Allows some localization | Fully localizable |

## Good Examples

### Example 1: Language
**Input**: "This is a piece of cake!"

**Expected Output**: Replace with "This is straightforward"

**Why it works**: Avoids idiom that doesn't translate

---

### Example 2: Imagery
**Input**: Only Western imagery

**Expected Output**: Diverse imagery representing multiple cultures

**Why it works**: Inclusive representation

---

## Poor Examples

### Example 1: Idioms
**Input**: "Break a leg!"

**Problem**: Doesn't translate, may cause offense

---

### Example 2: Stereotypes
**Input**: "As we all know in Western culture..."

**Problem**: Assumes everyone shares Western perspective

---

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Exemplary - All criteria met with excellence |
| 4 | Proficient - Meets most criteria completely |
| 3 | Competent - Basic requirements met |
| 2 | Developing - Some criteria met but incomplete |
| 1 | Beginning - Few or no criteria met |

---

## Quick Check

- [ ] Did I avoid idioms and slang?
- [ ] Is imagery diverse?
- [ ] Did I avoid cultural stereotypes?
- [ ] Are colors appropriate for target region?
- [ ] Is date/time format appropriate?
- [ ] Did I avoid assuming universal norms?
