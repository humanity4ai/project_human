# Age-Inclusive Standards

## Key Standards

### ISO 25556:2025
**Ageing societies — General requirements and guidelines for ageing-inclusive digital economy**

Published: May 2025
- General requirements for ageing-inclusive digital services
- Focus on older persons in digital economy
- Principles for inclusive design

### ISO/IEC Guide 71:2014
Guidelines for accessibility standards addressing the needs of older persons

### WCAG 2.2 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)
- Success Criterion 1.4.3: Contrast (Minimum) - 4.5:1
- Success Criterion 1.4.4: Resize text - 200%
- Success Criterion 2.5.5: Target Size - 44x44px minimum

## NIST Usability Guidelines for Older Adults

### Vision
- Minimum 16px font (18-20px recommended)
- High contrast (4.5:1 minimum)
- Avoid pure white backgrounds (can cause glare)
- Clear visual hierarchy

### Motor Control
- 44-48px minimum touch targets
- Adequate spacing between targets (8px+)
- Avoid time-limited interactions
- Provide multiple ways to complete actions

### Cognition
- Clear, simple language
- Short paragraphs
- Consistent navigation
- Progress indicators for multi-step tasks
- No assumption of prior knowledge

### Technology
- Don't assume tech literacy
- Provide clear instructions
- Offer help and support
- Allow mistakes and easy correction

## Best Practices

### Font Guidelines
- Body text: 16-20px minimum
- Line height: 1.5x
- Letter spacing: normal
- Sans-serif fonts preferred

### Color Guidelines
- 4.5:1 contrast ratio (WCAG AA)
- 7:1 for AAA
- Avoid red/green combinations
- Provide customization options

### Interaction Guidelines
- 44x44px minimum targets
- No time limits or long timeouts
- Clear feedback
- Easy undo/redo
