# Neurodiversity-Aware Design — Standards and References

## Primary Standards

### W3C COGA — Making Content Usable (2023)
- Objective 1: Help users understand what things are and how to use them
- Objective 2: Help users find what they need
- Objective 3: Use clear and understandable content
- Objective 4: Help users avoid mistakes and correct them
- Objective 5: Help users focus
- Objective 6: Ensure processes do not rely on memory
- Objective 7: Provide help and support
- Objective 8: Support adaptation and personalization
- URL: https://www.w3.org/TR/coga-usable/

### WCAG 2.2 — Cognitive Accessibility Success Criteria
- 2.4.5 Multiple Ways (Level AA)
- 3.1.3 Unusual Words (Level AAA)
- 3.1.5 Reading Level (Level AAA)
- 3.2.3 Consistent Navigation (Level AA)
- 3.2.4 Consistent Identification (Level AA)
- 3.3.2 Labels or Instructions (Level A)

## Clinical Frameworks (For Context, Not Diagnosis)

### ADHD — DSM-5-TR Diagnostic Criteria (2022)
- Inattention: 6+ symptoms for ≥6 months (ages ≤16: 6; ≥17: 5)
- Hyperactivity-impulsivity: Same threshold
- Several symptoms present before age 12
- Symptoms present in 2+ settings
- URL: https://www.psychiatry.org/psychiatrists/practice/dsm

### Autism Spectrum Disorder — DSM-5-TR (2022)
- Persistent deficits in social communication
- Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior
- Severity levels 1-3 based on support needs
- High co-occurrence with ADHD (~50%), anxiety, depression

### Dyslexia / Specific Learning Disorder — DSM-5-TR (2022)
- Difficulties learning academic skills for ≥6 months
- Reading, written expression, or mathematics
- Affects ~5-15% of population depending on language orthography
- Note: Dyslexia presentation differs by script — Chinese dyslexia involves different neural pathways than alphabetic dyslexia

## Cross-Cultural Neurodiversity

### Diagnostic Variation
- ADHD diagnosis rates: US ~9.4%, France ~0.5% (cultural diagnostic thresholds differ)
- Autism understanding varies: In some cultures, autism may be hidden due to stigma (Grinker, 2007, Unstrange Minds)
- Dyslexia in non-alphabetic scripts: Chinese dyslexia involves morphological and orthographic processing differences (Siok et al., 2004, Nature, 431, 71-76)

## Participatory Design

### Nothing About Us Without Us Principle
- Design WITH neurodivergent users, not just ABOUT them
- Include neurodivergent designers and researchers in the design process
- Avoid designing solely from clinical/medical framework

## Limitations
- Neurodivergent traits exist on spectrums — no two individuals with the same diagnosis are identical
- Co-occurrence is common (e.g., ADHD + dyslexia, autism + ADHD)
- These are design patterns, not clinical recommendations
- Cultural context shapes how neurodivergent traits are expressed and accommodated
