# Standards

## Emotional Intelligence Framework

### Core Components

1. **Self-Awareness**
   - Recognize your own emotions
   - Understand how you affect others
   - Know your strengths/limits

2. **Self-Management**
   - Manage emotional reactions
   - Stay composed under pressure
   - Adapt to changing situations

3. **Social Awareness**
   - Read emotional cues
   - Understand group dynamics
   - Show genuine interest in others

4. **Relationship Management**
   - Connect effectively
   - Inspire and influence
   - Manage conflict

## Empathetic Communication Standards

### The HEAR Model

**H - Halt**: Stop what you're doing, give full attention

**E - Engage**: Make eye contact, show you're present

**A - Anticipate**: Prepare to listen without judgment

**R - Replay**: Reflect back what you heard

## Authentic vs Performative Empathy

### Authentic Empathy
- "I can only imagine how difficult this is"
- "From what you've shared, it sounds like..."
- "I want to make sure I understand"
- "I hear you saying..."

### Hollow/Performative
- "I understand how you feel" (when you can't)
- "I know exactly what you're going through"
- "It could be worse"
- "At least..."

## Key Principles

### 1. Lead with Emotion
Always acknowledge feelings before content:
- "That sounds really difficult"
- "I can hear how frustrated you are"

### 2. Validate Before Solving
Solution-seeking is natural but premature:
- First: acknowledge
- Then: ask if they want solutions
- Finally: offer if wanted

### 3. Be Specific
Generic sympathy feels hollow:
- Name the specific emotion
- Reference specific details
- Show you really heard

### 4. Match Tone
Mirror emotional intensity:
- High emotion → quiet, warm, present
- Medium → acknowledgment + path forward
- Low → gentle encouragement
