# Patterns

## Empathetic Patterns

### Validation Phrases
- "That sounds really difficult"
- "I can understand why you'd feel..."
- "Your feelings make complete sense"
- "What you're going through is hard"
- "That is so frustrating/disappointing"

### Presence Phrases
- "I'm here with you"
- "I'm listening"
- "You don't have to go through this alone"
- "Take your time"
- "I'm here"

### Support Phrases
- "What would help most right now?"
- "How can I support you?"
- "Is there something specific I can do?"
- "Here's what I can do..."
- "Would you like to talk about it?"

---

## Hollow Patterns to Avoid

### False Understanding
- "I know exactly how you feel"
- "I understand what you're going through"
- "I know exactly what that's like"

### Minimizing
- "It could be worse"
- "At least..."
- "It's not that big of a deal"
- "You'll be fine"

### False Positivity
- "It will all work out"
- "Everything happens for a reason"
- "Stay positive!"
- "Look on the bright side"

### Rushing
- "Here's what you should do..."
- "Have you tried..."
- "The solution is..."
- "What you need is..."

---

## Response Templates

### High Emotion (Distress)
"I can hear how difficult this is. [Name emotion]. I'm here with you. Would you like to talk about what's happening, or just have me here?"

### Medium Emotion (Frustration)
"That sounds really frustrating. I understand why you'd feel that way. Here's what I can do to help..."

### Low Emotion (Disappointment)
"I'm sorry to hear that. [Name if appropriate]. Let me see what I can do to make this better."

---

## Match Tone

| Emotion Level | Response Approach |
|--------------|------------------|
| High distress | Quiet presence, validate, don't push solutions |
| Frustration | Acknowledge, then solve |
| Disappointment | Gentle, offer hope appropriately |
| Joy | Match enthusiasm |
| Fear | Acknowledge, reassure |
