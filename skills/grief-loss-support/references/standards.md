# Grief and Loss Support Standards

## Overview

Professional standards for providing non-clinical grief and bereavement support in digital content.

## Key Standards

### APA Grief Guidelines

The American Psychological Association recommends:
- Acknowledge the loss specifically
- Avoid minimizing statements
- Allow space for the person's unique grief journey
- Use the deceased's name when appropriate

Reference: https://www.apa.org/topics/grief

### SAMHSA Bereavement Guidelines

The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration provides:
- Non-prescriptive support language
- Validation of all grief responses
- Resource-aware responses

Reference: https://www.samhsa.gov/

### WHO Grief Support

World Health Organization guidelines on grief:
- Grief is a normal response
- No "right way" to grieve
- Support without rushing the process

## Clinical Boundaries

This skill provides **non-clinical** support. It does not:
- Diagnose complicated grief
- Provide therapy or counseling
- Replace professional mental health care

## When to Escalate

### Crisis Indicators
- Expressed desire to die
- Self-harm references
- Overwhelming hopelessness
- Inability to function

## Peer-Reviewed Research

- Stroebe, M. & Schut, H. (1999). The dual process model of coping with bereavement: Rationale and description. Death Studies, 23(3), 197-224. — Foundational oscillating model of grief — loss-oriented vs restoration-oriented coping.
- Neimeyer, R.A. (2001). Meaning Reconstruction and the Experience of Loss. American Psychological Association. — Evidence for meaning-making as a central grief process.
- Klass, D., Silverman, P.R. & Nickman, S.L. (1996). Continuing Bonds: New Understandings of Grief. Taylor & Francis. — Evidence that maintaining bonds with the deceased is normal, not pathological.
- Kastenbaum, R. (2012). Death, Society, and Human Experience (11th ed.). Pearson. — Critical examination of Kubler-Ross stage theory — developed for the dying, not the bereaved; not empirically sequential.
- Shear, M.K. (2015). Complicated grief. New England Journal of Medicine, 372(2), 153-160. — Clinical overview of prolonged grief disorder criteria.
