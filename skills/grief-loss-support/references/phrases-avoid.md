# Phrases to Avoid

## Common Clichés

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| They're in a better place | Minimizes current pain |
| At least they're not suffering | Dismisses the loss |
| Time heals all wounds | Rushes grief |
| I know how you feel | Invalidates unique grief |
| Be strong | Suppresses emotions |
| It could be worse | Compares losses |
| At least you have... | Minimizes loss |
| They lived a long life | Age doesn't diminish loss |
| Everything happens for a reason | Unsolicited meaning |
| You'll find someone else | Dismisses relationship |

## What to Say Instead

- "I'm so sorry."
- "This is so hard."
- "I wish I had words."
- "Tell me about them."
- "I'm here with you."

## Phrases That Assume

- "You should be over it by now"
- "You need to move on"
- "Try to focus on the positives"
- "Be grateful for what you had"

These assume grief has a timeline or solution.
