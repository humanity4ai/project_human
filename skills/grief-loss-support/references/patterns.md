# Supportive vs Harmful Phrases

## Phrases That Help

| Use This | Not That |
|----------|----------|
| I'm sorry for your loss | They're in a better place |
| This is so hard | Time heals all wounds |
| Take all the time you need | You should be over it by now |
| I'm here with you | I know how you feel |
| There are no words | At least they're not suffering |
| What can I do? | You need to move on |
| Your grief is valid | It could be worse |

## Minimizing Phrases to Avoid

- "They're in a better place"
- "At least they're not suffering"
- "Time heals all wounds"
- "You should be over it by now"
- "I know how you feel"
- "Be strong"
- "It could be worse"
- "At least you have other children/family"
- "They lived a long life"

## Prescriptive Phrases to Avoid

- "You need to move on"
- "You should focus on the positives"
- "Try to look on the bright side"
- "Be grateful for what you had"
- "Grieve but don't dwell"

## Presence-First Language

**Good**: "I'm here with you in this."
**Bad**: "Let me fix this for you."

**Good**: "This is so hard."
**Bad**: "Cheer up."

**Good**: "Tell me about them."
**Bad**: "Don't be sad."
