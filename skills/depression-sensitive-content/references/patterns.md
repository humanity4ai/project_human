# Patterns to Avoid

## Stigmatizing Language Patterns

### Terms That Shame

| Avoid | Prefer |
|-------|--------|
| Suffering from | Living with, experiencing |
| Afflicted with | Dealing with, coping with |
| Victim of | Person experiencing |
| Mentally ill (noun) | Person with mental illness |
| Crazy, insane, psycho | Using person-first language |
| Psycho, nut, loony | Avoid entirely |
| Committed suicide | Died by suicide |

### Phrases That Minimize

| Avoid | Prefer |
|-------|--------|
| It's all in your head | That sounds really difficult |
| Just get over it | How can I support you? |
| Think positive | What helps you feel better? |
| Others have it worse | Your feelings are valid |
| Snap out of it | I'm here for you |

### Clinical Misstatements

| Avoid | Prefer |
|-------|--------|
| Disease | Condition, disorder (clinical); experience, situation (casual) |
| Mental illness | Mental health condition (when appropriate) |
| Patient | Person, individual (non-clinical contexts) |
| Treatment | Support, help, strategies |
| Diagnosis | Experience, situation |

## Cognitive Friction Patterns

### Complex Sentences

**Avoid**: "Depression, which is characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, loss of interest, and difficulty concentrating, affects millions of people worldwide and requires professional intervention for management."

**Prefer**: "Depression affects millions of people. It can feel overwhelming, but support is available."

### Negative Framing

**Avoid**: "Don't let depression control your life."

**Prefer**: "You can take steps toward feeling better."

### Jargon

**Avoid**: "Non-compliance with treatment protocols"

**Prefer**: "Finding it hard to follow the treatment plan"

## Patterns That Increase Hope

### Strength-Based

**Good**: "People with depression can and do recover."

**Avoid**: "Depression is chronic and lifelong."

### Resource-Oriented

**Good**: "There are many support options available."

**Avoid**: "Treatment is difficult to access."

## Summary Checklist

- [ ] No shaming verbs (suffering, afflicted, victim)
- [ ] No clinical terms in casual contexts
- [ ] No minimizing language
- [ ] Short sentences (under 20 words)
- [ ] Positive framing when possible
- [ ] Person-first language
- [ ] Crisis resources included when relevant
